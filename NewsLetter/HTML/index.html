DHL FORUM

DHL FORUM DRAWS WASHINGTON, D.C. ELITE

After a one-year hiatus, the DHL Public Policy Forum (now known as the DHL Forum) returned to Washington, D.C., in May 2017. Hosted by Roger Libby, Executive VP of Corporate Public Policy for DPDHL Americas, and his D.C.-based team, the event was a unique opportunity for DHL senior leadership to engage customers, vendors and business and government partners in a timely dialogue on new disruptions to international trade and commerce. Addressing seismic shifts in both U.S. and global political landscapes – and their impact on global logistics and supply chains – the Forum covered topics including:

  • How new media’s changing coverage of events in Washington is impacting business and government policy.

DHL PRODUCTS

THE BENEFITS OF DHL SHIPMENT VALUE PROTECTION

Shipment Value Protection (SVP) gives you added peace of mind, knowing your goods – particularly high-value items – are covered in the event of physical loss and/or damage, including from natural disasters and other unforeseen threats. With this service, your shipment is secure from the moment DHL takes possession until final delivery. It’s that simple!

U.S. Secretary of Commerce Wilbur Ross speaks at the DHL Forum

  • Disruptions to the digital economy, delivery services and U.S. regulations.

  • Global security changes, shifting alliances and co-creation

Speakers included U.S. Senator Jeff Flake (R-AZ), Mexico’s U.S. Ambassador Gerónimo Gutiérrez, Steve Clemons (editor of The Atlantic magazine) and author and ABC News contributor Kristin Soltis Anderson. U.S. Chamber of Commerce President and CEO Tom Donohue offered a private-sector perspective, and Sen. Rand Paul (R-KY) shared insights on U.S. legislation and world events.

Delivering the keynote address was U.S. Secretary of Commerce Wilbur Ross, highlighting the who’s who of political and business leaders, policy makers and global strategists in attendance.

DID YOU KNOW?

DEPT. OF DEFENSE AWARDS DHL NEW CONTRACT

In April, Team Polar/ DHL Express was awarded a contract to provide Next-Generation Delivery Service (NGDS) for the U.S. Department of Defense. The deal, part of a $2 billion government-wide, multi-vendor contract to consolidate federal package delivery, ensures contract continuity for the core DHL services through at least 2023.
